A delay in the semiconductor supply chain caused the production of smartphones to stall. The production stall led to a shortage of inventory for major retailers. Because of the inventory shortage, retailers could not meet the high consumer demand during the holiday season. The missed demand resulted in a 30% drop in quarterly revenue. Consequently, the company's stock price plummeted. The plummeting stock price triggered a series of layoffs in the marketing department. Reduced marketing staff led to a decline in brand visibility. Finally, the decline in brand visibility caused a permanent loss of market share to competitors.
